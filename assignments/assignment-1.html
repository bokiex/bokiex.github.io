Assignment 1: Board Game Prototype - Designer Diary

This assignment required us to come up with a board game prototype after allowing us to test out the different types of board game mechanics present through playing many types of board games in class

We have to pen down our design ideas and train of thought throughout the design process. The different concepts we had before and what we included/excluded.

The Million Dollar Idea

I joined the group a little late for this assignment as I thought I had a group. They were already discussing the idea and had a general sense of what the game was going to be like when I joined.

The game is a board/card game. It's like a card game dungeon crawler with linear progression in levels. It is based off of the many card games online and we got a few card designs and ideas from the different card games as well. The basis of it is that you build your deck along as you go through the levels, creating synergies and getting better cards to fight stronger monsters.

Design Process

We took it to Discord to discuss about the design. We wanted the essence of a card game to stay while implementing board game mechanics. Together with a hint of randomness in everything to spice it up a little.

We decided that the level design should have a linear progression with a boss fight at the end. Just a regular dungeon gameplay to reach the top and defeat the final boss.

This simple level design allowed us to focus on card mechanics and design. We put in a lot more thought into the card design in terms of synergy between cards and the different element themes that you can follow to build a deck.

Difficulties

One of the first issues we had was balancing the game. Of course that goes for all games, but I was pretty inexperienced in balancing card games so it was quite a tough thing for me to pick up. We countered this by dictating how much 1 mana is worth. Then, we add more attributes based on the element of the card. For example, Fire cards have more damage, Water cards have more healing and Earth cards have more block.

The second bigger issue was the Random Number Generator (RNG) aspect of the game. After playtesting our first prototype, we've come to realise that our game didn't promote much strategising and didn't encourage smart plays. A lot of the game is based on luck, which made it a little unfun for players trying to win.

The third one is gameplay itself. Our game focuses on deckbuilding, and Mr Oon raised a good question on whether there is a winning deck. A deck that no matter what, will always result in a win. We realised there might be some issues with overpowered decks and combos that grants basically immortality.

Lastly, the deckbuilding aspect of the game was kind of lacklustre in the sense that there wasn't much opportunities to gain and discard cards. That resulted in the huge variety of cards to be untapped throughout the entire game and the starting cards playing a bigger impact on the game rather than the other cards. Combos and synergies were also harder to get and set up as there were very little cards to work with, so unless you're lucky, there's no chance of getting the cards you need for a good combo.
